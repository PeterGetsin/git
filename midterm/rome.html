Overview of Ancient Rome

Ancient Rome was a powerful empire that ruled much of Europe, the Middle East, and North Africa. Known for its architecture, military prowess, and political innovations, Rome had a lasting influence on the modern world.

Key Contributions

  • Development of Roman law
  • Construction of roads and aqueducts
  • Introduction of the concept of republican government

Important Figures

  • Julius Caesar - General and dictator
  • Augustus - First emperor of Rome
  • Marcus Aurelius - Philosopher-emperor
