Overview of The Maya

The Mayans were a Mesoamerican civilization that thrived from around 2000 BCE to 1500 CE. Known for their advanced knowledge of astronomy, mathematics, and their highly structured society, the Mayans built impressive cities across present-day Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and El Salvador.

Key Contributions

  • Mayan Calendar: The Mayans developed a sophisticated calendar system that included the Tzolk'in, a 260-day sacred calendar, and the Haab', a 365-day solar calendar.
  • Mathematics: The Mayans were one of the first civilizations to use the concept of zero in their mathematical system, which was essential for their complex calculations.
  • Mayan Writing: The Mayans developed a hieroglyphic writing system, which was used to record historical events, religious texts, and governmental affairs.

Important Figures

  • Pacal the Great: A ruler of the Maya city-state of Palenque, Pacal is known for his long reign and the construction of the Temple of the Inscriptions, where his tomb was discovered.
  • Lady Six Sky: A powerful queen of the Maya city-state of Naranjo, she is celebrated for her leadership and military achievements.
  • Yax Kuk Mo: The first ruler of the Copán Maya civilization, he is credited with establishing the dynastic lineage that would rule Copán for several centuries, helping to solidify the city's importance as a major center of Mayan culture and politics.
