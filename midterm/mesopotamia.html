Overview of Mesopotamia

Mesopotamia, known as the cradle of civilization, developed along the Tigris and Euphrates rivers. It was here that the first cities and writing systems emerged, laying the foundation for future civilizations.

Key Contributions

  • Invention of the wheel
  • Development of cuneiform writing
  • Creation of one of the first legal codes (Code of Hammurabi)

Important Figures

  • Gilgamesh - King of Uruk, legendary hero of the Epic of Gilgamesh
  • Sargon of Akkad - Founder of the Akkadian Empire
  • Hammurabi - King of Babylon, known for his code of laws
