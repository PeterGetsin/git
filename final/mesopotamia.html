Overview of Mesopotamia

Mesopotamia, known as the cradle of civilization, developed along the Tigris and Euphrates rivers. It was here that the first cities and writing systems emerged, laying the foundation for future civilizations.

Key Contributions

  • Cuneiform Writing: Mesopotamians developed one of the earliest systems of writing, known as cuneiform, which was used for record-keeping, literature, and administrative purposes.
  • The Code of Hammurabi: One of the earliest and most complete written legal codes, created by King Hammurabi of Babylon around 1754 BCE.
  • The Wheel: Mesopotamians are credited with the invention of the wheel around 3500 BCE, which revolutionized transport and labor.

Important Figures

  • Gilgamesh: A legendary king of Uruk, featured in the "Epic of Gilgamesh," one of the oldest works of literature.
  • Sargon of Akkad: Founder of the Akkadian Empire, one of the world's first empires, uniting northern and southern Mesopotamia.
  • Hammurabi - King of Babylon, known for his code of laws
